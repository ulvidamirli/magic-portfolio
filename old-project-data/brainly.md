## Design Process
### Interviews
We conducted interviews with 20 students who had recently experienced exam pressure to understand their needs and pain points in the preparation process. Participants were selected based on varied academic performance and backgrounds to capture a comprehensive view of their experiences.

FINDING 1
Students reported feeling overwhelmed by conflicting expectations from parents and peers.

FINDING 2
Many expressed uncertainty about what to study or how to structure their preparations.

FINDING 3
Time management was a significant challenge, with students struggling to balance study time and other commitments.

### Surveys
Following the interviews, we distributed a survey to over 500 students to quantify their study habits and preferences. The survey aimed to gather insights on their existing study tools, frequency of use, and specific challenges faced during exam preparation.

- 80% of students prefer automated study tools over manual input methods.
- 60% of respondents reported feeling overwhelmed during exam seasons.
- About 55% of students indicated a need for daily reminders to keep them on track.

### Usability Testing
We conducted usability testing with 15 students to evaluate the app's interface and functionality. The focus was on the test prep creation flow and quiz management features, using a combination of think-aloud protocols and task-based scenarios to gather detailed feedback.

Problem 1
Users found it challenging to navigate between quiz management options.

Solution 1
We streamlined the interface by consolidating options into a unified management dashboard.

### Learnings:
- Understanding user emotions is critical in designing stress-reducing features.
- Automation in study tools significantly improves user engagement.
- Frequent reminders enhance commitment to study schedules.
